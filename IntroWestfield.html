Introducing the parish of Westfield

Who are we

Posted by Eleanor Jackon on August 24, 2023

Westfield was originally exactly that, an area of farms west of Radstock on the Fosse Way, stretching along the Roman highway. It was built in a AD49 to link Exeter to Saint Albans and later to London and Lincoln.

The A367 remains an arterial Road but the original Road took a diversion to avoid the swamp in the middle of Radstock and re-emerged behind Methodist Church. Some stretches can still be seen.

Everything changed when Coal began to be mined commercially from 1763, with two major collieries being developed at Norton hill (a scene of a terrible mine disaster in 1908 which led to modern safety measures such as the Davy lamp) and at the Wellsway.

Bath College, on the site of the mines' manager's mansion was founded in 1947 to train local young people to work in the mines and on the farms. Today it is IT!

All the mines were closed by 1973 but much of the wellsway mine can still be seen in a small industrial estate, while Norton hill disappeared under housing developments, although its railway station is now a heritage museum.

Manufacturing units also left, with only one large print company remaining. The parish council produced a neighbourhood plan after much consultation with residents in 2015-17. One aim was to maintain as much employment land as possible since at present 60% of residents have to out commute to Bristol bath or Shepton Mallet.

The parish council, having recently declared a climate emergency and taking green issues very seriously indeed, endeavour to educate residents in the issues and have purchased this year three fields along the Riverside with the intention of developing major trails and conservation projects.

One distinctive feature of the area is the batch heaps of coal waste which have rewilded and have been planted with trees to stabilise them. They have to be checked annually against slippage since water is flowing out of the disused mines.

In 2011 Westfield parish council was created under the terms of the local government act. There were approximately 5063 adults on the electoral roll but the publication of the 2021 census result will give a better indication of numbers, given how many of the once substantial Polish community in Westport have returned home. There are 11 councillors on the parish council and two unitary authority councillors. There is a clerk and assistant to whom the community to whom the community are much indebted for the smooth administration of the recreation grounds.
