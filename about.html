What is Westfield in Bloom

Westfield in Bloom is a network of councillors and volunteers who put together and enter into South West in Bloom, building on the success of individual projects in the "It's Your Neighbourhood" section.

So congratulations are due to the members of Westfield in Bloom, a network of enthusiatic gardeners and volunteers who worked together with Rob Wicke (responsible for the hanging baskets and model coal tricks) and Westfield Parish Council to achieve a 'silver gilt' award in the annual SW in Bloom competition.

Since it started in 1963, Britain in Bloom has grown into the biggest horticultural campaign in Europe. ommunities across Brtaincombine horticultural skills, enthusiasm and fun t oimporve an regenerate our local environments through imaginiatvie planting of trees, shrubs, flowers and landscaping, conservation and recycling projects, and ot sweep away the eyesores that blight our streets such as litter, graffiti and vandelism.

Under the auspices of the Royal Horticultural Society, the Britain in Bloom campaign judging criteria is being revised but is still focused around three core pillars: but with more emphasis on community.

So, with this in mind, we stitch together the "It's Your Neighbourhood" entries with some add-ons and contruct a route for the judging. Plus, some litter picking. It is very important to construct a narrative around atheme. We have done "Westfield, a community of ordinary people achieving extraordinary things.", which we are, and now we are doing "Gardening is good for you: the health and benefits of community gardening"

The judging usually takes about three hours, starts with an introduction presentation and ends with a cup of tea. The aim is to introduce as many volunteers as possiebl, and generally showcase Westfield. The areas are as follows:

  • The Waterside Project run by Westfield Parish Council and Volunteers
  • Westfield Allotments and Garde Society / Larch Court
  • West Hill paren and recreation area run by the Parish Council
  • Elm Tree Avenue shops, jubile garden and the war memorial stone
  • Westfiled Primary School
  • Fosseway School
  • Swallows HQ office gardens
  • Shakespeare Road residents

This is all organised by a convenor (Eleanor Jackson at present), a treasurer (Cllr Pat Williams), an informal commitee of Cllr Ron Hopkins and Paul Millard, with support from Juliette Millard and Cllr Diana Cooper, but we are separate from the Parish Council.
